Planet Facts

**Planet Order from the Sun; Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus and Neptune**

Mercury
Discovery: Known to the ancients and visible to the naked eye.
Named for: Messenger of the Roman gods.
Diameter: 3,031 miles (4,878 km).
Orbit: 88 Earth days.
Day: 58.6 Earth days
picture; http://factslegend.org/30-facts-mercury/



Earth
Diameter: 7,926 miles (12,760 km)
Orbit: 365.24 days
Day: 23 hours, 56 minutes
picture; http://www.softicons.com/object-icons/earth-icons-by-pinchodesigns/earth-3-icon


Jupiter
Discovery: Known to the ancients and visible to the naked eye.
Named for: Ruler of the Roman gods.
Diameter: 88,730 miles (428,400 km).
Orbit: 11.9 Earth years.
Day: 9.8 Earth hours.
picture; http://pics-about-space.com/planet-jupiter-png?p=1

Saturn
Discovery: Known to the ancients and visible to the naked eye
Named for: Roman god of agriculture
Diameter: 74,900 miles (120,500 km)
Orbit: 29.5 Earth years
Day: About 10.5 Earth hours
picture; http://pics-about-space.com/planet-saturn-png?p=3



Uranus
Discovery: 1781 by William Herschel (was thought previously to be a star)
Named for: Personification of heaven in ancient myth
Diameter: 31,763 miles (51,120 km)
Orbit: 84 Earth years
Day: 18 Earth hours
picture; http://pics-about-space.com/planet-uranus-png?p=1

Neptune
Discovery: 1846
Named for: Roman god of water
Diameter: 30,775 miles (49,530 km)
Orbit: 165 Earth years
Day: 19 Earth hours
picture; http://space-facts.com/neptune/


Pluto (Dwarf Planet)
Discovery: 1930 by Clyde Tombaugh
Named for: Roman god of the underworld, Hades
Diameter: 1,430 miles (2,301 km)
Orbit: 248 Earth years
Day: 6.4 Earth days
picture; http://pics-about-space.com/planet-pluto-png?p=1
